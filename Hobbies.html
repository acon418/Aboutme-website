My Gym Routine

Here, I'll outline my exercise routine. On each of the five days of the week that I train, I do pull, push, legs, cardio, and full body exercises. You are under no obligation to use my fitness plan; it is entirely optional. Do whatever it takes to achieve your goal.

Sunday Monday Tuesday Wednesday Thursday Friday Saturday
Back/Biceps Chest/Arms Quads/Glutes Cardio Full body Rest Rest

  • Sunday: Back/Biceps Exercises

    1. Lat-pull Downs 4 sets of 12
    2. Barbell-Rows (PRONATED) 5 sets of 10
    3. Chin-ups 3 sets Up to Failure
    4. Dumb-bell curls 3 sets of 10
    5. Deadlifts 5 sets of 10
    6. Power Shrugs (with Dumbbells) 3 sets of 12

  • Monday: Chest/Arms Exercises

    1. Barbell Bench Press (Flat or Incline) 5 sets of 10-8-6-3-3
    2. Military Press (Full Lockout)3 sets of 12
    3. Weightes Dips
    4. DB Flys (Flat or Incline Full ROM) 3 sets of 12
    5. Facepulls (Cable, Arms across each other at start)3 sets of 12
    6. Sull-Crushers 3 sets of 10

  • Tuesday: Quads/Glutes

    1. Front-Squats 5 sets of 12-10-8-6-3
    2. Leg Extensions 4 sets of 12
    3. Hamstring Curls (DB or Machine) 4 sets of 12
    4. Calf Raises (Body weight or Machine) 4 sets of 20
    5. RDL'S (with DB for a better Stretch and ROM) 4 sets of 10
    6. Leg-Press Machine (WIDE FEET=INNER QUADS, LOW FEET=OVERALL QUADS, NARROW FEET=OUTER QUADS, HIGH FEET=GLUTES & HAMS) 5 sets of 12
    7. Back-Squats 5 sets of 12-10-8-6-3

  • Wednesday: Cardio

    • If you have access to a gym, I would suggest using the treadmill for two hours of running or a good, moderate pace on the bike. If you don't have time to go to the gym, spend two hours running outside (MUST HAVE APPROPEAT RUNNING SHOES). Stretch for 20 minutes after you've run.


  • Thursday: Full body

    1. Barbell-Rows (PRONATED) 5 sets of 10
    2. Barbell Bench Press (Flat or Incline) 5 sets of 10-8-6-3-3
    3. Front-Squats 5 sets of 12-10-8-6-3
    4. Dumb-bell curls 3 sets of 10
    5. Hamstring Curls (DB or Machine) 4 sets of 12
    6. Military Press (Full Lockout) 3 sets of 12

      7. This is totally optional! You can choose your top 6 or 7 exercises on your full body day, every now and then you can switch it up if you like. It's all about your personal preference and the goals that you're trying to accomplish at the gym or at home.


  • Friday/Saturday: Rest Day

    • Stretching helps me relax any tension or soreness from the previous night's sleep, therefore I usually like to do it first thing in the morning on my rest days. Additionally, it aids in boosting blood circulation and gets your body ready for the day. You can avoid waking up in additional agony by stretching your muscles before bed.

    REST PERIODS

    No more than 3-5min between different exercises, no more than 20 seconds between "REST-PAUSE" sets, and no more than 10 seconds between drop sets.

    WORKOUT DURATION

    Try to be out of the gym as quickly as possible, to increase your work capacity and room for maximum recovery. so no more than 60-130min per session.
